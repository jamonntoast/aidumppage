Overview: The Core Principles

This section covers the most important concepts that determine tax obligations. Understanding these principles is the first step in assessing your parents' situation. The key factors are their residency status for tax purposes and Australia's rules on foreign income.

1. Australian Resident for Tax Purposes

This is the most critical factor. It is different from their visa or citizenship status. Since they are permanent residents and live in NSW, the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) will almost certainly consider them "Australian residents for tax purposes."

2. The Worldwide Income Rule

Australian residents for tax purposes are taxed on their worldwide income. This means they must declare all income they earn from all sources, both inside and outside Australia. This includes income from China.

3. Do They Need to Lodge a Tax Return?

Yes, if their total worldwide income is above the tax-free threshold (currently $18,200 AUD). As they are over 70, they may be eligible for the Seniors and Pensioners Tax Offset (SAPTO), which increases their effective tax-free threshold. A tax professional must calculate this.

How Information is Shared: DTA & CRS

It is crucial to understand that tax authorities have robust systems for sharing information across borders. This is not a guess; it is a formal, automated process. The two main pillars of this system are the Double Taxation Agreement (DTA) and the Common Reporting Standard (CRS).

Australia and China have a DTA designed to prevent double taxation and fiscal evasion. A key part of this agreement is the "Exchange of Information" article. This allows the ATO to make a specific request to China's tax authority (State Taxation Administration) for information about a specific taxpayer, such as details of their income or assets.

This is the more powerful, automatic system. Both Australia and China are signatories. Under the CRS, Chinese banks and other financial institutions must review their accounts, identify those held by Australian tax residents, and automatically report the account details and financial information (like interest earned and account balances) to the ATO every year. This is a bulk data exchange, not a specific request.
